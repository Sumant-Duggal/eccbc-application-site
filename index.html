Perspective

Visibility drives execution.

In large-scale FMCG operations, the biggest constraint is rarely strategy, it is visibility. When thousands of daily decisions across sales, distribution, and service are made with incomplete or delayed information, even the best plans struggle to translate into execution.

Digital transformation, for me, is not just about new tools, but about making operations measurable, connected, and responsive in real time.

Core Idea

In Practice (AB InBev)

This is the problem I have worked on at AB InBev, within Drinks Dispense operations in the UK&I. The function had recently been brought in-house and lacked a unified view of performance across installations, capital expenditure, and field operations.

I led the development of automated data pipelines and Power BI dashboards that integrated previously fragmented data sources, enabling leadership to track key operational and financial metrics with far greater speed and accuracy.

Building on this foundation, I focused on improving execution on the ground. I designed and implemented a route optimisation tool for a field force of over 150 technicians, improving productivity and resource allocation. I also led the development of DraftVision, a digital platform that replaced manual, unstructured communication with a standardized workflow for service and installation requests. This not only reduced inefficiencies but also created a structured data layer that allowed us to generate insights and unlock new revenue opportunities.

Foundation (Skills & Approach)

Alongside my professional experience, my MSc in Business Analytics and Big Data at IE University has strengthened my ability to work across the full data value chain, from building pipelines in Python and SQL to translating outputs into clear, actionable insights for business stakeholders.
